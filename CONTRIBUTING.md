# Contributing to the Universal Information Standard

The Universal Information Standard (UIS) is an open specification developed collaboratively by contributors from various organizations and backgrounds. We welcome participation from anyone interested in advancing structured information representation.

## How to Contribute

### Areas of Contribution

Contributions to UIS can include:

- **Specification development**: Writing, reviewing, and refining specification text
- **Terminology review**: Ensuring consistent and clear definitions
- **Design principle evaluation**: Assessing the principles guiding UIS design
- **Conformance testing**: Developing conformance criteria and test methodologies
- **Editorial improvements**: Enhancing clarity, consistency, and accessibility
- **Cross-reference verification**: Ensuring internal consistency across sections

### Contribution Process

1. **Review existing materials**: Familiarize yourself with the current specification
2. **Identify areas for improvement**: Review open discussions and issues
3. **Prepare your contribution**: Draft proposed changes with clear rationale
4. **Submit for review**: Create an issue or pull request explaining your proposal
5. **Engage in discussion**: Respond to feedback and refine your contribution
6. **Reach consensus**: Work toward agreement among stakeholders

## Style Guidelines

### Writing Style

UIS documentation follows the conventions of international standards:

- Use precise, unambiguous language
- Employ consistent terminology throughout
- Favor declarative statements over prescriptive implementation guidance
- Use numbered lists for requirements and conformance criteria
- Include rationale where concepts may be counterintuitive

### Formatting Conventions

- Section numbers use three-digit prefixes (e.g., 001, 002)
- Requirements are labeled with "shall" for mandatory criteria
- Recommendations use "should" for preferred but optional criteria
- Permissions use "may" for allowed but not required criteria
- Capability statements use "can" for potential capability

## Working Groups

The UIS specification may be developed through working groups focused on specific topics. Working groups operate under the oversight of the editorial committee.

## Communication

- **Issues**: Use GitHub Issues for bug reports, clarification requests, and proposals
- **Discussions**: Use GitHub Discussions for general questions and brainstorming
- **Pull requests**: Use PRs for specification text changes

## Recognition

All contributors to UIS are recognized in the specification's acknowledgments section. By contributing, you agree to have your name or organization listed.

## Code of Conduct

All participants in the UIS community are expected to follow our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Please review it before participating.

## License

By submitting contributions to UIS, you agree that your contributions will be released under the Apache License 2.0 and will be included in the specification without additional restrictions.
