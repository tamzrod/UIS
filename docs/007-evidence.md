# Evidence

## 8.1 Overview

Evidence provides the mechanism by which assertions within UIS are supported or substantiated. This clause defines the concepts, structure, and requirements for evidence within the information framework.

## 8.2 Fundamental Concepts

### 8.2.1 Evidence Definition

Evidence is information that provides support for or substantiation of an assertion. Evidence establishes grounds for belief in the truth or validity of a claim.

### 8.2.2 Assertion Definition

An assertion is a statement that something is the case. Assertions may include:

- Factual claims about information objects or their properties
- Statements about relationships between entities
- Claims about provenance or origin
- Statements of compliance or conformance
- Permissions, obligations, or other deontic statements

## 8.3 Evidence Types

### 8.3.1 Direct Evidence

Evidence that directly establishes a fact:

- Documented observations
- Recorded measurements
- Original sources
- Primary documents

### 8.3.2 Derivative Evidence

Evidence derived from other sources:

- Citations and references
- Transcriptions
- Summaries
- Interpretations

### 8.3.3 Attestational Evidence

Evidence provided by authoritative sources:

- Certifications
- Endorsements
- Approvals
- Witness statements

### 8.3.4 Computational Evidence

Evidence generated through processing:

- Calculated results
- Analytical outputs
- Systematic derivations

## 8.4 Evidence Structure

Evidence consists of:

a) **Assertion**: The claim being supported

b) **Evidence content**: The supporting information

c) **Evidence source**: The origin of the evidence

d) **Evidence strength**: Indication of reliability or weight

e) **Evidence validity**: Time period of applicability

## 8.5 Evidence Relationships

Evidence may be related to:

- The assertions they support
- Other evidence in supporting chains
- Information objects to which they relate
- Agents providing or attesting to evidence

## 8.6 Evidence Evaluation

Evidence evaluation considers:

a) **Source reliability**: The trustworthiness of the evidence origin

b) **Content relevance**: The logical connection to the assertion

c) **Content sufficiency**: Whether the evidence adequately supports the assertion

d) **Temporal appropriateness**: Whether the evidence is applicable at the relevant time

e) **Independence**: Whether the evidence is free from conflicts of interest

## 8.7 Evidence Chains

Complex assertions may require chains of evidence where:

- Evidence supports intermediate assertions
- Multiple evidence items contribute to a conclusion
- Evidence from multiple sources is aggregated

UIS supports the representation of evidence chains without prescribing specific evaluation methods.

## 8.8 Conformance

Evidence information conforms to UIS if it:

1. Identifies the assertion(s) it supports

2. Provides sufficient information to locate or access the evidence

3. Indicates the source of the evidence

4. Documents the evidence type according to 8.3

5. Supports evaluation according to 8.6
