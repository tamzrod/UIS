# Provenance

## 9.1 Overview

Provenance provides information about the origin, custody, and history of information. This clause defines how provenance is represented within the UIS framework.

## 9.2 Provenance Definition

Provenance encompasses:

a) **Origin**: Where information came from

b) **Custody**: Who has held or processed the information

c) **History**: What changes have occurred over time

d) **Justification**: Why the information exists in its current form

## 9.3 Provenance Entities

### 9.3.1 Agents

Agents are entities that act or have authority to act. Agents include:

- **Persons**: Individual human beings
- **Organizations**: Groups of persons or other organizations
- **Systems**: Automated or computerized processes
- **Software**: Programs or applications

### 9.3.2 Activities

Activities are actions or events that involve information. Activities include:

- **Creation**: Initial generation of information
- **Transformation**: Modification of information
- **Derivation**: Creation of new information from existing sources
- **Communication**: Transmission of information
- **Access**: Retrieval or use of information

### 9.3.3 Entities

Entities are the things involved in provenance. Entities include:

- Information objects
- Agents
- Activities
- External resources

## 9.4 Provenance Relationships

Provenance is expressed through relationships between entities:

a) **Was generated by**: Links information to the activity that created it

b) **Was influenced by**: Links information to factors that affected it

c) **Was associated with**: Links activities to agents involved

d) **Used**: Links activities to entities they utilized

e) **Was derived from**: Links derived information to its sources

## 9.5 Provenance Properties

Provenance information may include:

a) **Timestamp**: When an activity occurred

b) **Duration**: How long an activity took

c) **Rationale**: Why an activity was performed

d) **Method**: How an activity was performed

e) **Outcome**: What resulted from an activity

## 9.6 Scope of Provenance

Provenance may be captured at various levels:

### 9.6.1 Object Provenance

Information about a specific information object:

- Who created it
- When it was created
- What it was derived from
- How it has changed

### 9.6.2 Property Provenance

Information about specific properties:

- When a property was added
- Who modified a property
- What the previous value was

### 9.6.3 Relationship Provenance

Information about relationships:

- When a relationship was established
- Who created the relationship
- Why the relationship exists

## 9.7 Provenance Quality

Provenance quality attributes include:

a) **Completeness**: Whether all relevant provenance is captured

b) **Accuracy**: Whether provenance accurately reflects reality

c) **Accessibility**: Whether provenance is available when needed

d) **Security**: Whether provenance is protected from tampering

e) **Interpretability**: Whether provenance can be understood

## 9.8 Conformance

Provenance information conforms to UIS if it:

1. Identifies agents according to 9.3.1

2. Identifies activities according to 9.3.2

3. Documents relationships according to 9.4

4. Records applicable properties according to 9.5

5. Is captured at an appropriate scope according to 9.6

6. Is associated with the information object(s) it describes
