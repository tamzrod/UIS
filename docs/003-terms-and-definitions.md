# Terms and Definitions

## 1. General

This clause establishes the vocabulary used throughout the Universal Information Standard (UIS). Terms are defined in a domain-neutral manner consistent with their use in the specification.

Where terms are defined elsewhere in UIS, cross-references are provided. Where terms have established meanings in other contexts, this clause notes any intentional deviations.

The requirement language used in this specification follows the conventions of RFC 2119, as specified in [002-normative-references.md](002-normative-references.md).

## 2. Fundamental Terms

### 2.1 information

Knowledge concerning objects, such as facts, events, things, processes, or ideas, including concepts that within a context have a particular meaning.

*Note 1*: Information can be represented through various forms including text, numbers, images, sounds, and combinations thereof.

*Note 2*: Information acquires meaning through interpretation within a specific context.

### 2.2 representation

A concrete form or encoding of information suitable for communication, processing, or storage.

*Note*: A representation is a physical manifestation of information in a specific format.

### 2.3 structured information

Information that has been organized according to a defined model or schema.

*Note*: Structured information permits consistent interpretation and processing by defined mechanisms.

### 2.4 interoperability

The ability of two or more representations to exchange information and use the exchanged information.

*Note*: Interoperability may exist at syntactic, semantic, or organizational levels.

## 3. Information Model Terms

### 3.1 information model

An abstract representation of the structure and semantics of information.

*Note*: An information model defines concepts, relationships, and constraints independently of specific implementations.

### 3.2 entity

A distinguishable thing or concept that can be represented within an information model.

*Note*: Entities include information objects, agents, activities, and resources.

### 3.3 information object

A discrete unit of structured information with defined identity.

*Note*: An information object is characterized by its properties and its relationships to other entities.

### 3.4 property

A characteristic or attribute of an entity that describes some aspect of the entity.

*Note*: Properties have values that may be simple or structured.

### 3.5 value

A specific instance of data assigned to a property.

*Note*: Values may be atomic or structured.

### 3.6 type

A classification that defines a category of entities with common characteristics.

*Note*: Entities may conform to one or more types.

### 3.7 identifier

A sequence of characters that uniquely identifies an entity within a given context.

*Note*: Identifiers enable references between entities.

## 4. Relationship Terms

### 4.1 relationship

A connection or association between two or more entities.

*Note*: Relationships have semantics that define the nature of the connection.

### 4.2 source

The entity from which a relationship originates.

### 4.3 target

The entity toward which a relationship is directed.

### 4.4 relationship type

A classification of relationships that share common semantics.

## 5. Provenance Terms

### 5.1 provenance

Information about the origin, custody, and history of an entity.

*Note*: Provenance supports assessment of quality and trustworthiness.

### 5.2 agent

An entity (person, organization, or system) that acts or has authority to act.

### 5.3 activity

An action or event that results in the creation, modification, or destruction of entities.

## 6. Evidence Terms

### 6.1 evidence

Information that supports or substantiates an assertion.

*Note*: Evidence provides grounds for belief in the truth of a claim.

### 6.2 assertion

A statement that something is the case.

*Note*: Assertions may include factual claims, permissions, or other statements requiring substantiation.

## 7. Versioning Terms

### 7.1 version

A specific state of an entity at a point in time.

### 7.2 revision

A modification to an entity that results in a new version.

### 7.3 version history

The sequence of versions of an entity over time.

## 8. Conformance Terms

### 8.1 conformance

Satisfaction of the requirements specified in UIS.

### 8.2 conforming representation

A representation that satisfies all applicable requirements of UIS.

### 8.3 implementation

A concrete realization of a conforming representation.

*Note*: Implementations may use various technologies and formats.

## 9. Abbreviations

**UIS**: Universal Information Standard

*Note*: Other abbreviations may be defined in subsequent clauses as required.
