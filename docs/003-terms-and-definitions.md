# Terms and Definitions

## 3.1 General

This clause establishes the terminology used throughout UIS. Terms are defined in a domain-neutral manner consistent with their use in the specification.

Where terms are defined elsewhere in UIS, cross-references are provided. Where terms have established meanings in other contexts, this clause notes any intentional deviations.

## 3.2 Fundamental Terms

### 3.2.1 information

Knowledge concerning objects, such as facts, events, things, processes, or ideas, including concepts that within a context have a particular meaning.

*Note 1*: Information can be represented through various forms including text, numbers, images, sounds, and combinations thereof.

*Note 2*: Information acquires meaning through interpretation within a specific context.

### 3.2.2 representation

A concrete form or encoding of information suitable for communication, processing, or storage.

*Note*: A representation is a physical manifestation of information in a specific format.

### 3.2.3 structure

The organization or arrangement of components within information.

*Note*: Structure defines how elements of information relate to one another.

### 3.2.4 semantic

Concerned with meaning, especially meaning in language or information.

## 3.3 Information Model Terms

### 3.3.1 information object

A discrete unit of structured information with defined identity.

*Note*: An information object is characterized by its properties and its relationships to other information objects.

### 3.3.2 property

A characteristic or attribute of an information object that describes some aspect of the object.

*Note*: Properties have values that may be simple or complex.

### 3.3.3 value

A specific instance of data assigned to a property.

*Note*: Values may be atomic or structured.

### 3.3.4 identifier

A sequence of characters that uniquely identifies an information object within a given context.

### 3.3.5 type

A classification that defines a category of information objects with common characteristics.

*Note*: Information objects may conform to one or more types.

## 3.4 Relationship Terms

### 3.4.1 relationship

A connection or association between two or more information objects.

*Note*: Relationships have semantics that define the nature of the connection.

### 3.4.2 source

The information object from which a relationship originates.

### 3.4.3 target

The information object toward which a relationship is directed.

### 3.4.4 relationship type

A classification of relationships that share common semantics.

## 3.5 Provenance Terms

### 3.5.1 provenance

Information about the origin, custody, and history of information.

*Note*: Provenance supports assessment of information quality and trustworthiness.

### 3.5.2 agent

An entity (person, organization, or system) that acts or has the authority to act.

### 3.5.3 activity

An action or event that results in the creation, modification, or destruction of information.

### 3.5.4 entity

Something that exists and can be represented. Entities include information objects, agents, and activities.

## 3.6 Evidence Terms

### 3.6.1 evidence

Information that supports or substantiates an assertion.

*Note*: Evidence provides grounds for belief in the truth of a claim.

### 3.6.2 assertion

A statement that something is the case.

*Note*: Assertions may be factual claims, permissions, or other statements requiring substantiation.

### 3.6.3 assertion type

A classification of assertions that share common characteristics.

## 3.7 Versioning Terms

### 3.7.1 version

A specific state of an information object at a point in time.

### 3.7.2 version identifier

A unique designation for a specific version of an information object.

### 3.7.3 revision

A modification to an information object that results in a new version.
